What is a parasite

Parasite is a word branched from the latin roots of para alongside and sitos food, the combined meaning of the roots make up our current definition of parasite; An organism that lives benefiting of another at the other’s expense.

What is a parasitoid

A parasitoid is defined as an insect whose larvae that lives as a parasite within another living organism until it hatches, at which point it devours the host.

what & how

The Ampulex compress is known often as the emerald cockroach wasp, an insect with a killer ability to disable the basic survival functions of its prey: the cockroach.

It accomplishes this by delivering two sequential toxins, one to the stomach area that temporarily disable front leg motor function, and a secondary toxin directly to the brain of the cockroach that almost inebriates’ basic instinctual functions of the prey, often referred by humans as the freeze, flight, fight response.
Once this is done the wasp is thought to judge the potency of its work by tasting the blood of the victim for concentration of toxin. Once, the wasp is satisfied it pulls upon the prey’s antennae to follow which the cockroach obeys, similarly to a dog on a leach.

Once the prey is brought to the wasp’s burrow, it lays a white larvae egg within the prey abdomen. For this reason, the compressa is considered to be a parasitoid.

To prevent potential escape, the compress blocks the burrow from the outside with debris and leaves. Inside, during the larvae’s first week it slowly feeds off of the prey from the inside, until it grows out of its cocoon and leaves the cockroach carcass for good.
